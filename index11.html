üîë Cryptographic Erase

Cryptographic Erase (or crypto-shredding) is a method of data destruction that makes target data irrecoverable by destroying the cryptographic keys used to encrypt it. Instead of physically overwriting or destroying the storage media, you simply eliminate the key. Without the key, the encrypted data is rendered permanently unintelligible, effectively deleting it.

‚óè

Fast & Efficient: Destroying a small key is much faster than overwriting terabytes of data.

‚óè

Secure: Renders data useless on lost or stolen devices, or when decommissioning hardware.

‚óè

Media Agnostic: Works on all types of storage, including SSDs where physical overwriting is complex.
